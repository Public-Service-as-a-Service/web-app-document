# Impeccable — Design Context

This file captures the design context for **Dokumenthantering** (Sundsvalls kommun's document management application). Every impeccable skill consults this before doing design work. Keep it current.

---

## Design Context

### Users

A broad municipal audience, not a single persona:

- **Department heads and document-responsible staff** who touch the system frequently and own document structures, revisions, and organizational hierarchy.
- **Case workers, HR staff, and administrators** who process registrations (employment certificates, personnel records, decisions) as part of larger workflows.
- **Occasional users from many departments** who open the tool a few times a month to search, upload, or look something up — it is unfamiliar territory each time.

The design must serve both power-admins who live in the tool AND occasional visitors who arrive with no context — without patronizing either. Clarity wins ties. Don't assume prior knowledge; don't waste repeat users' time.

**Primary viewing context**: office workstations during the workday. Light mode primary. Swedish primary (i18n: `sv` default, `en` secondary).

### Brand Personality

**Warm, human, approachable** — *a government tool that doesn't feel like one.*

Three-word voice: **civic, warm, considered.**

Emotional goal: the user should feel *taken care of* — the interface anticipates, explains, and stays out of the way. Not cold, not bureaucratic, not childish. Confident without shouting.

**References**:
- **Notion / Craft** — warm document-centric apps. Readable typography, soft surfaces, quiet rounding, friendly without being twee.
- **Gov.uk / Entur** — public-sector digital done right. Calm, legible, accessibility-first, prose-first explanations, high-visibility focus states.

**Anti-references**:
- Generic shadcn admin dashboards (the current baseline is fine but not the destination).
- "AI slop": cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, gradient text, left-border accent stripes, glowing cards, sparklines as decoration, identical card grids.
- Over-designed consumer SaaS vibes. This is civic infrastructure, not a startup.
- Cold, clinical government software that feels like punishment to use.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Rethink the shell + commit to a point of view — disciplined by UX and accessibility.**

The ambition is a design that feels unmistakably *this* product: Notion's warmth meets Gov.uk's civic seriousness, through a Nordic typographic lens. Stop short of visual flourish that doesn't serve a function. Every choice earns its place.

**Theme**: Light primary. Dark mode maintained as a peer but secondary. WCAG 2.1 AAA body contrast (≥7:1) where feasible; AA minimum elsewhere. This exceeds DOS-lagen legal requirements.

**Color palette** (OKLCH, tinted neutrals, Sundsvall heritage preserved as accent):
- Background / surface: warm off-white (cream-tinted, never pure `#fff`)
- Ink / foreground: deep warm graphite (near-black, never pure `#000`)
- Primary action: muted civic blue — a reinterpreted Sundsvall blue, deeper and desaturated so it reads as institution rather than SaaS
- Secondary accent: warm sage/olive for positive states, badges, document-type markers
- Warning: warm ochre (not neon)
- Focus ring: high-contrast amber (Gov.uk-inspired) — prominent, legible, signature
- 60-30-10 visual weight: 60% warm neutral / 30% ink and secondary text / 10% color

**Typography** (Nordic direction, zero reflex fonts):
- UI + body: **Hanken Grotesk** (variable, Söhne-adjacent, open source, generous x-height, Nordic feel)
- Long-form / editorial moments (document-type descriptions, empty states, prose explanations): **Source Serif 4** (variable, refined)
- Monospace (registration numbers, codes, metadata): **Geist Mono** (clean, modern — not the reflex IBM Plex or Space Mono)
- Banned: Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta, Fraunces, Newsreader, Playfair, IBM Plex, Space Grotesk (they are the current AI monoculture)
- Modular scale, fixed `rem` for UI, fluid `clamp` for marketing-style content. Minimum 1.25 ratio between steps.

**Layout & space**:
- Warm off-white canvas with quiet structure. Not a cards-within-cards dashboard.
- Asymmetric where it serves legibility — e.g. wide content column + narrow metadata rail rather than equal thirds.
- Break the grid for emphasis (a single oversized registration number on a document detail page, not five stat tiles).
- Body copy capped at 65–75ch.
- Varied spacing for hierarchy — tight groupings, generous separations. 4pt scale, semantic tokens.

**Motion**:
- Already well-tokenized in `globals.css` (`--motion-ease-out-quart`, view-transitions for directional nav, staggered list reveals). Keep and extend; honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- One well-orchestrated moment beats ten micro-interactions.
- No bounce/elastic. Exponential easing only.

### Design Principles

Five principles that should guide every design decision on this project:

1. **Civic craft** — This is a public institution's tool, made with the care of an editorial product. Every surface respects the user's time. No decoration for decoration's sake; every flourish earns its place by clarifying or delighting.

2. **Typography does the work** — Visual hierarchy comes from type weight, size, and rhythm — not from boxes, borders, or left-side stripes. If a heading, a number, and a label can communicate priority without a decorative container, prefer that. Chrome is quiet; content is the hero.

3. **Warm neutrality, one confident accent** — Backgrounds and ink are tinted (never pure `#fff`, never pure `#000`). Color appears sparingly and meaningfully. One confident accent beats five timid ones. Heritage Sundsvall blue earns its place as a civic action color, not a default.

4. **Accessible first — beautiful *because* accessible** — WCAG 2.1 AAA where feasible. Focus indicators are prominent (amber ring, Gov.uk-inspired) and never hidden to "look cleaner." Reduced motion honored. Touch targets ≥44px. Plain Swedish microcopy. These constraints shape the aesthetic; they do not limit it.

5. **Respect the document** — Registration numbers, document metadata, and previews are the hero. Long-form descriptions breathe. Empty states invite, not apologize. The tool disappears; the document appears.
